### Decision Rules

| Situation | Action |
| :--- | :--- |
| Entering a market with incumbents | **Status Quo Challenge**; find the offline friction digital hasn't solved. |
| Launching a marketplace | **Single-Sided First**; aggregate demand before acquiring supply. |
| Defining OKRs/KPIs | **Objective vs Result**; focus on controllable inputs, not just outputs. |
| Pitching an idea | **Pitch-Deck-as-Credibility**; state who you are and what you want upfront. |
| Expanding globally | **Globalization = Localization + Scale**; adapt context, don't just translate. |
| Facing an existential crisis | **Crisis Risk-Taking**; increase calculated risk to break the deadlock. |
| Defining company purpose | **P-P-P Framework**; Purpose → Product → Profit. |
| Evaluating a business model | **Linear vs Platform**; does it create feedback loops or one-off transactions? |
| Team is fragmented | **Pin vs Domain**; pick one specific problem and commit. |
| Team is stagnant | **Internal Open Source Model**; let devs self-select tasks. |

### Trade-off Tables

| Concept | Option A | Option B | Trade-off |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Thinking** | Linear | Exponential | Linear provides certainty & stability; Exponential offers scale & disruption but looks flat initially. |
| **Control** | Centralized | Decentralized | Centralized ensures quality/guarantees; Decentralized leverages distributed intelligence & scales better. |
| **Hiring** | Senior/Specialist | Generalist/Malleable | Seniors bring proven skills but rigid "inside boxes"; Generalists adapt quickly but require more coaching. |
| **Culture** | Cultural Fit | Cultural Enrichment | Fit reduces friction & speeds up early execution; Enrichment boosts long-term innovation but increases cognitive load. |
| **Metrics** | Lag Factors (Results) | Lead Factors (Objectives) | Lag confirms past success; Lead steers future decisions but requires faith without immediate proof. |
| **Growth** | Rapid Scaling | Slow Growth | Rapid scaling captures market share but risks structural rigidity; Slow growth allows pivots before breaking. |
| **Competition** | Competitive Positioning | Organic Positioning | Competitive focuses on beating rivals (finite game); Organic focuses on self-improvement (infinite game). |
| **Business Model** | Transaction | Ads/Visibility | Transaction monetizes core value (matching); Ads monetize scale but risk alienating users if overdone. |
| **Communication** | Impression | Impact | Impression measures reach (vanity); Impact measures behavioral change (value). |
| **Team Structure** | Large Teams (Domains) | Small Teams (Pins) | Large teams cover more ground but suffer fragmentation; Small teams move fast but have narrower scope. |

## Trade-Off Tables

**Principles vs. Data**
| Criteria | Principle-First | Data-Driven |
|---|---|---|
| Speed | Fast (axiom application) | Slow (data collection) |
| Accuracy under novelty | High (if principles hold) | Low (past patterns fail) |
| Risk | Wrong principles compound | Analysis paralysis |
| Best for | Strategic, repeated decisions | Tactical, novel situations |

**Platform vs. Product**
| Criteria | Platform | Product |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue model | Indirect, via third-party growth | Direct, from own users |
| Time to value | Longer | Shorter |
| Scale ceiling | Exponential (network effects) | Linear (own capacity) |
| Risk | Empty platform | Feature creep, scope bloat |

**Debt vs. Equity**
| Criteria | Debt | Equity |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | Interest payments | Ownership dilution |
| Control | Retained | Shared |
| Obligation | Fixed repayment | No repayment |
| Best for | Predictable cash flow | High-growth, uncertain returns |
