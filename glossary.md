# Terms (alphabetical)

**0 Problem (مسئله صفر)** — The root constraint underlying a system (e.g., liquidity for marketplaces, trust for platforms). Stripping away symptoms until you find the single constraint preventing movement. (Ch01)

**Arman (آرمان)** — Purpose as a visionary ideal that transcends daily tasks; deeper than a temporary *maqsad* (goal) or *resalat* (mission). (Ch12)

**Barn-Raising** — Community members collectively pause their work to help one family; illustrates how social pressure + shared identity mobilizes action faster than monetary incentives. (Ch03)

**Commoditization (کامدیتیزیشن)** — Treating all suppliers as interchangeable, competing only on price. Destroys differentiation, loyalty, and platform stickiness. (Ch11)

**Construct–Deconstruct–Construct** — Break a system into components, analyze independently, reassemble into something novel. (Ch08)

**Crisis Risk-Taking Model** — The inverse relationship between risk and survival in a crisis: doing nothing is the highest risk; taking a bigger, calculated risk is the only path forward. (Ch13)

**Cross Margin** — Commission-based revenue model. Software platforms approach zero marginal cost per additional transaction. (Ch02)

**Decomposition** — Breaking a system into components, analyzing independently, then reassembling into something novel. (Ch08)

**Design Thinking** — Questioning *what the problem even is* before proposing fixes; push past symptoms by drilling into root causes. (Ch01, Ch08, Ch10)

**Digikala** — Iranian e-commerce platform; example of building rails others ride on rather than replacing manufacturers. (Ch05)

**Engineer Trap** — Seeing a problem and immediately reaching for the first obvious solution—usually a bad idea. (Ch01)

**Engineered Purpose** — A fabricated mission statement designed to motivate; inevitably fails when challenges arise. (Ch12)

**Empowerment (امپاورمنت)** — The foundational principle that if my suppliers grow, I grow; building platforms where supplier success is the primary metric. (Ch11)

**Exit as Strategy** — Exiting isn't losing; it's choosing not to play anymore. Treat it as a deliberate decision, not a failure state. (Ch07)

**Exponential vs Linear Thinking** — Linear thinkers see gradual rise and conclude "won't scale"; exponential thinkers recognize compounding growth creates step-changes that bypass conventional wisdom. (Ch03, Ch05)

**First Principles Thinking** — Strip away inherited assumptions; rebuild from fundamental truths. Ask "what must be true?" instead of "how did we always do this?" (Ch06, Ch08, Ch10)

**Flywheel** — Self-reinforcing cycle where each iteration makes the next faster/cheaper. (Ch02, Ch16)

**Generous Tit-for-Tat** — Mirror opponent's move but occasionally forgive betrayal to avoid lock-in from noise. (Ch04)

**Generosity** — Cooperate first; mirror opponent's previous move. (Ch04)

**Gross Unit Economics** — Direct profitability per transaction before overhead. Must be positive to scale sustainably. (Ch02)

**Habit Formation (عادت‌سازی)** — Whoever captures user habit wins; platform strategy is ultimately about shaping and owning habits. (Ch11)

**Iceberg of Competition** — Visible layer (features, pricing) vs. invisible layer (how you define winning, who you compare yourself to). (Ch07)

**Independent** — Self-sufficiency achieved *through* interdependence, not in spite of it. (Ch20)

**Infinite Game** — Interactions where players expect to meet again repeatedly; enables cooperation. (Ch04)

**Interdependence** — Being mutually dependent such that one's gain creates the other's gain; prerequisite for real independence. (Ch20)

**Jihadi Entrepreneurship** — Forward motion without a complete plan ("tell me where to go and I'll get there"). (Ch06)

**Jihadi Resistance** — Saying "it can't be done" as a defense mechanism against change. (Ch06)

**Liquidity** — The single most critical metric for marketplaces; without it, pricing, quality, and trust collapse. (Ch03)

**Magnet Hire** — A candidate whose primary value is their network and ability to attract other talent. (Ch15)

**Moving Average of Culture** — The actual culture is the aggregate of recent team actions, not aspirational documents. (Ch15)

**Muk (مک)** — Short-termism; optimizing locally while destroying systemic value long-term (e.g., adding roads → more traffic). (Ch01)

**Network Effect Reversal** — Growth drivers that eventually become growth inhibitors (e.g., Facebook connections → privacy loss → lower engagement). (Ch01)

**Novelty vs Technology** — Novelty is a new method of doing things; technology is the actual underlying capability. Novelty cannot be defended; technology can. (Ch09)

**Obligation (رسالت)** — Permanent calling; unlike goals, it cannot be completed, so it provides lasting alignment. (Ch20)

**Objective vs Result Distinction** — Results = revenue, margin, market share. Objectives = customer focus, solution quality, distribution quality. Don't confuse the two. (Ch07)

**OODA Loop** — Observe–Orient–Decide–Act: military decision cycle adapted for business strategy. (Ch08)

**Organization Design** — Organizations that survive require systems, not heroics. (Ch07)

**P24 Acceptance Principle** — "Every empowered squad empowers other squads to the extent possible." (Ch20)

**Phase** — Temporary target; once achieved, it must be replaced. (Ch20)

**Platform Thinking** — Empower and inspire others to deliver value rather than delivering it yourself. (Ch04, Ch10)

**Platform vs Aggregator (پلتفرم در برابر اگریگیتور)** — An aggregator merely aggregates value; a platform creates conditions where others create value. (Ch11)

**Positive Feedback Loop** — Compounding mechanism behind network effects; small advantages become exponential. (Ch09)

**Principle-First (اصل اول)** — Start with the fundamental belief that "if my suppliers grow, I grow." Build everything from this premise. (Ch11)

**Principle-First Thinking** — Make decisions based on foundational truths rather than lagging metrics. (Ch17)

**Productivity** — A product's success is dictated by how well it communicates its value to the user, not just its underlying code. (Ch13)

**Professional Judgment** — 80% of professional judgment relies on communication skills, not just technical expertise. (Ch13)

**Purpose** — A deeply held, internal conviction that naturally motivates action, as opposed to an artificially constructed goal. (Ch12)

**Quality Assurance** — Comes from reputation and selection, not gatekeeping. (Ch03)

**Reframing Over Solving** — Often the real leverage is changing the problem definition, not solving the stated problem. (Ch01)

**Resalat (رسالت)** — Mission; permanent calling that cannot be completed. (Ch20)

**Reverberation** — A product's success is dictated by how well it communicates its value to the user, not just its underlying code. (Ch13)

**Ripple** — A product's success is dictated by how well it communicates its value to the user, not just its underlying code. (Ch13)

**Search-Centric Value** — A marketplace's core value is search (Uber = driver search, Airbnb = accommodation search, Amazon = product search). (Ch18)

**Second-Order Thinking** — Ask "what happens after that?" to uncover hidden consequences and unintended side effects. (Ch01)

**Self-Management** — Organizations that survive require systems, not heroics. (Ch07)

**Self-Sufficiency** — Achieved *through* interdependence, not in spite of it. (Ch20)

**Silent Partner** — Someone who believes in the principles acts on their own; no need to keep reminding them. (Ch19)

**Single-Sided vs Two-Sided** — Single-sided platforms have inherent constraints. (Ch04)

**Slow Growth** — Deliberate, foundational growth over rapid scaling, allowing for pivots before structural rigidity sets in. (Ch13)

**Status Quo Challenge** — Question every "this is how it's always been done." Ask who benefits and whether that benefit still justifies it. (Ch08)

**Strategy** — Emerges from the decisions a company makes across operations, finance, and growth — not from a document. (Ch02)

**Synergy** — Combined output exceeds sum of parts; requires win-win mindset. (Ch20)

**Systemic Thinking** — Systems create their own constraints; adding capacity often increases demand, creating negative feedback loops. (Ch01)

**Test-and-Defect** — Probe once, then commit to Tit-for-Tat if opponent responds cooperatively, otherwise defect. (Ch04)

**Tit-for-Tat** — Cooperate first; mirror opponent's previous move. (Ch04)

**T-Shirt Test** — A validation metric where both the team and the customers would proudly wear the company's purpose on a t-shirt. (Ch12)

**Total Return** — A product's success is dictated by how well it communicates its value to the user, not just its underlying code. (Ch13)

**Trust Deficit** — The primary vulnerability of any marketplace business model. (Ch13)

**Value Chain Upward Trace** — Map where value accumulates after your activity. (Ch04)

**Value Creation** — True value comes from transforming the core function, not just improving form (Gutenberg's press didn't make better type—it removed the printing process from manual labor). (Ch10)

**Working Capital ≠ Venture Money** — Working capital finances inventory/receivables with low risk; venture equity finances high-risk growth. Never mix them. (Ch16)

**Zero Problem (مسئله صفر)** — The root constraint underlying a system. (Ch01)


# Culture is Communication — Glossary

**Axiomatic Principles** — Foundational truths a team agrees upon upfront so new problems can be solved without re-debating basics (Ch65, Ch70)

**Ambassador Effect** — A transformed person becomes a multiplier, spreading change outward (Ch71)

**Ambassador of Change** — Treat every interaction as an opportunity to transform someone's worldview, not just make a binary accept/reject decision (Ch71)

**Belief Asymmetry** — Startups' real advantage over incumbents isn't agility; it's that incumbents hold opposite beliefs about what's possible (Ch74)

**Blue Ocean Strategy** — Seek white-space markets rather than competing in crowded spaces where consumers already have solutions (Ch66)

**Born-Again Principles** — Bake openness, cloud-first, platform thinking into the product from day one; don't bolt them on later (Ch64)

**Character of a Product** — A product reflects the moral character and worldview of its creator (Ch72)

**Chicken-and-Egg Problem** — The classic marketplace dilemma; solve one side first, often by playing single-player mode temporarily (Ch71)

**Common Ground Requirement** — You cannot effectively critique someone without first establishing shared philosophical premises (Ch74)

**Composition Advantage** — Innovation is often arranging existing pieces differently rather than inventing new ones (Ch64)

**Correlation Hunting** — Finding patterns in data without understanding cause; describing, not explaining (Ch70)

**Counter-Intuitive Scaling** — Doing things that don't scale to build early momentum before applying levers (Ch67)

**Culture as Communication** — Culture isn't a poster or mission statement; it's the cumulative effect of every interaction, shaping how people feel when they enter and leave (Ch67, Ch71)

**Customer Obsession** — Deeply understand user needs, but don't let their stated preferences override your judgment (Ch67)

**Design Philosophy** — Product decisions are fundamentally moral/philosophical choices, not just usability problems (Ch72)

**Design Thinking** — Not solving the stated problem, but reframing it so the user arrives at the right conclusion organically (Ch63)

**Empathy-First Problem Solving** — Frame problems around the audience's lived experience, not your own assumptions (Ch68)

**Endorsement by Absence** — Silence is data; no response means the framing didn't land (Ch68)

**Equity Financing** — Raising capital by selling ownership shares; no repayment obligation; shared risk/reward (Ch75)

**Exceptional User** — An outlier whose problems are magnified; useful for revealing hidden product dimensions (Ch63)

**Exit Feeding Input** — Startup success is cyclical, not linear; exits must feed back into new ventures (Ch62)

**First Principles Thinking** — Reason from fundamentals, not borrowed opinions; ask "why" recursively until the assumption collapses (Ch67, Ch70)

**Founder Mentality** — Distributed ownership and decision-making across the team, not concentrated in one person (Ch63)

**Free-Riding** — Natural and necessary condition for platforms; users consume value without direct payment (Ch71)

**Functional Utility** — The actual value a product provides to users, independent of technical sophistication (Ch64)

**General Practitioner Mindset** — Looking at the whole system to heal the patient, rather than treating isolated symptoms (Ch73)

**Growth Mindset** — The belief that people can become better than they currently are; opposite of Fixed Mindset (Ch72)

**Incumbent Blindness** — The deeper the specialization, the harder it is to see outside the frame; expertise becomes a blocker (Ch74)

**Individual Development Plan (IDP)** — A personalized coaching roadmap targeting a person's skill gaps (Ch63)

**Instrumental Thinking** — Treating people as resources to optimize rather than humans capable of growth (Ch72)

**Innovation Incubators** — Artificial environments that isolate innovators from prevailing norms; useful historically but not always optimal (Ch64)

**Killer App** — The one application so essential it drives adoption of the entire platform (Ch71)

**Law of Diminishing Returns** — Recognizing when incremental efforts yield decreasing value; pivot before hitting the wall (Ch73)

**Macros vs Micros** — Macro trends (mobility, cloud, social) endure for decades; micro trends (hype) fade quickly (Ch67)

**Mental Architecture** — Structured reasoning needed to navigate chaos; without it, innovation devours you (Ch70)

**Narrative + Numbers** — Persuasion requires both a compelling story and concrete data; one without the other fails (Ch68)

**Network Effects** — A mediocre protocol wins if it builds a network; a brilliant protocol fails without adoption (Ch67)

**Network Value Before Network Effect** — A network must provide individual utility before it becomes valuable because others joined (Ch74)

**No Body / Embody** — Don't argue with assumptions; make them visible through your own design so inconsistency becomes undeniable (Ch72)

**Platform Thinking** — Build reusable infrastructure (plugins, extensions) rather than custom integrations; design for emergence (Ch63, Ch64, Ch66)

**Pleaser vs. Value** — Pleasing builds likability; delivering value builds impact; communicate to change minds, not just win approval (Ch69)

**Principle-First Thinking** — Start with foundational assumptions, not iterative guesses; if you lack first principles, you can't tell when you're wrong (Ch66, Ch70)

**Rational vs. Reason** — Economics equates rationality with profit-maximization; reason encompasses narrative, emotion, and meaning-making (Ch68)

**Relational Thinking** — Interaction-based dynamics focused on trust, autonomy, and long-term connection; opposite of Transactional Thinking (Ch73)

**Resource View vs. Competence View** — See people as fixed resources to deploy vs. seeing them as capable of growth and transformation (Ch72)

**Same Boat Relationship** — In platform businesses, you and your users are co-investors; their success is your success (Ch63)

**Single Player Mode** — Starting as a focused vertical product before expanding horizontally (Ch71)

**Signal vs Hype** — Macro trends survive because they're rooted in fundamental human conditions; micro trends die because they're surface-level (Ch67)

**Silicon Valley Flywheel** — Successful founders become angels/mentors/funders; ~10% success rate compounds the ecosystem (Ch62)

**Specialist Mindset** — Focusing exclusively on a narrow domain while ignoring broader impact; often blocks innovation (Ch73)

**Speed Over Precision** — Prioritize velocity over perfection in uncertain conditions with limited funding (Ch67)

**Status Quo Challenge** — The discipline of questioning established patterns; most effective when the audience is already doubting the default (Ch64, Ch69)

**Thinness Better** — Competing solely on operational efficiency or digital UX without a structural advantage; must be paired with a real moat (Ch75)

**Transactional Thinking** — Exchange-based interactions focused on immediate value or profit; opposite of Relational Thinking (Ch73)

**Trickle-Down Value** — Design products for the end user (doctor), not the purchasing organization (hospital), creating bottom-up pressure (Ch66)

**Value Creation Before Monetization** — Build the network first; observe what users already pay for, then extract a fraction (Ch62)

**Virtual Cycle** — Success driven by visionary storytelling rather than market-size calculations (Ch68)
