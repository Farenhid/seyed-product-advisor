# Chapter 4: Strategy is Culture

## Core Idea
Strategy emerges from the culture of interaction between players. In repeated, long-term games, cooperative cultures outperform competitive ones because mutual benefit compounds over time.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Tit-for-Tat**: Cooperate first; mirror opponent's previous move
  - When to use: Repeated interactions with known opponents
  - How: Start nice, respond directly, no grudges

- **Generous Tit-for-Tat**: Same as TFT but occasionally forgive betrayal
  - When to use: Environments with noise/false signals
  - How: After opponent defects, sometimes still cooperate to avoid lock-in

- **Test-and-Defect**: Probe once, then commit to Tit-for-Tat if opponent responds cooperatively, otherwise defect
  - When to use: Early-stage market entry
  - How: Test opponent's type, then follow their pattern

- **Platform Thinking**: Empower & inspire others to deliver value rather than delivering it yourself
  - When to use: Scaling beyond resource constraints
  - How: Identify value flows upward, build connections rather than vertical control

## Key Concepts
- **Infinite Game**: Interactions where players expect to meet again repeatedly; enables cooperation
- **Finite Game**: One-shot or terminal interactions; enables defection
- **Value Chain Upward Trace**: Map where value accumulates after your activity
- **Platform vs. Vertical**: Platform creates network effects; vertical owns delivery
- **Single-Sided vs. Two-Sided**: Single-sided platforms have inherent constraints
- **Value Shift**: Moving from one value proposition to another in a market
- **Core Value**: Initial offering that proves the model before expansion
- **Noise/Forgiveness**: Tolerance for misinterpretation in repeated games

## Mental Models
- **Win-Win Beats Win-Lose**: Maximizing partner's benefit reduces competition and self-harm
- **Nice First**: Always start cooperatively; never defect on first move
- **Forgiveness Prevents Lock-In**: Without occasional forgiveness, noise permanently destroys cooperation
- **Resistance Signals Opportunity**: Where people say "it can't be done," platform disruption is most likely

## Anti-patterns
- **Pure Competition**: Trying to maximize own win at opponent's loss; fails in infinite games
- **Vertical Delivery**: Taking responsibility for all value creation; caps growth at resource limits
- **Ignoring Value Flow**: Building without tracking where money/value moves upward
- **Over-Marketing a Weak Core**: Pushing marketing instead of fixing the initial value proposition

## Key Takeaways
1. Always start nice and never defect first—this property alone explains why the best strategies won Axelrod tournaments
2. Forgiveness matters: without it, noise permanently breaks cooperation in repeated games
3. Platform thinking means empowering others to deliver, not doing everything yourself
4. Trace the value chain upward to find where real value accumulates
5. Innovation lives where skeptics say "impossible"—that's where platforms emerge
6. Culture is strategy: being honest, reliable, and forgiving is a competitive advantage
7. Shift to core value first, then expand value propositions strategically
