# Chapter 15: Strategy is Culture

## Core Idea
Culture is defined by the moving average of recent actions, not written statements, and building a unique culture requires hiring malleable generalists who align with core values rather than rigid senior experts.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Culture as Action (Moving Average)**: Culture is not a mission statement; it is the moving average of actions taken over the last 90 days.
  - When to use: Evaluating or shaping team dynamics.
  - How: Observe actual behaviors (e.g., showing up on time) rather than reading value statements.
- **Generalist-First Hiring**: Early teams need unformed, fast-learning generalists, not specialized seniors.
  - When to use: Pre-product-market fit / early scaling stages.
  - How: Hire people without rigid "inside boxes" who can be molded into the unique culture you are building.

## Key Concepts
- **Magnet Hire**: A candidate whose primary value is their network and ability to attract other talent.
- **Cultural Fit**: The ultimate hiring metric; evaluated by requiring candidates to internalize core values before even starting the interview.
- **Unique Culture**: A culture so distinct that employees cannot easily replicate it elsewhere, reducing turnover.
- **Moving Average of Culture**: The actual culture is the aggregate of recent team actions, not aspirational documents.
- **Generalist (خمیرمایه)**: An unformed individual with high learning agility, ideal for early-stage adaptation.
- **Inside Box**: Rigid thinking patterns brought by overly specialized or senior candidates.
- **Negative Pride**: Holding strong conviction while remaining open to feedback; true humility.
- **Arrogant Ego**: Using past credentials (e.g., top universities/agencies) to dismiss others' input.

## Mental Models
- **Hiring for Malleability**: Early roles require clay-like people who can be shaped, not pre-carved statues.
- **Culture is Retention**: A unique culture creates a barrier to exit; generic cultures make employees easily replaceable elsewhere.
- **Values as Filters**: Use core values as a gatekeeping mechanism before investing time in interviews.

## Anti-patterns
- **Copy-Pasting Culture**: Adopting Netflix or Spotify values without adapting them to your context; leads to a generic, ineffective culture.
- **Senior-Only Hiring**: Bringing in highly specialized seniors early on who bring "inside boxes," resist pivots, and demotivate founders.
- **Value Statement Delusion**: Believing a written document defines your culture instead of looking at recent behavioral evidence.

## Key Takeaways
1. Hire early employees for learning speed and malleability, not rigid expertise or seniority.
2. Define your culture by the moving average of the last 90 days of actions, not by a mission statement.
3. Build a unique culture that cannot be replicated elsewhere to increase employee retention.
4. Make cultural alignment a prerequisite for interviews; reject candidates who don't adopt your core values upfront.
5. True humility means having strong convictions but actively seeking and acting on contradictory feedback.
6. Avoid arrogant hires who rely on past credentials (e.g., elite schools/agencies) to dominate conversations.
